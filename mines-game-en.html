Bankroll Management in Mines

Smart bankroll control is universally recognized as the single most important factor for a sustainable gaming experience. Regardless of your strategy, setting strict stop-loss and win-goal limits per session is paramount.

Using the Mines Demo Mode is highly recommended for practicing bankroll structures without any financial liability. It allows you to test how rapid changes in bet sizes or mine configurations affect your balance over 50 to 100 theoretical rounds before risking actual cash.

In terms of structured financial systems, players frequently adapt classic betting progressions like Martingale or Fibonacci:

  • The Martingale System: This strategy dictates doubling your bet size immediately following a loss, with the goal of recovering all previous losses and securing a small profit upon the next winning round. Note that this progression scales up exponentially and carries the risk of depleting a bankroll rapidly during an extended losing streak.
  • The Fibonacci System: A more gradual alternative, this system shifts bet sizes following a mathematical sequence where each bet is the sum of the two preceding ones. Following a win, you step back down the sequence, making it less punishing on your balance than the Martingale model.

Regardless of the system you apply, the golden rule remains to never chase losses and to always play with funds you can afford to lose.
